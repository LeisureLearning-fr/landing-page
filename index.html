Why this exists

I sat through an old school training. That's what pushed me to build something else.

My first mistake was selecting an online course from a well-known company. They got me good — while purchasing, the price swung from £1,300 to £700, and still I felt it was too expensive for the content quality.

Think of a close-to-retirement teacher coming to class because he has to, who has lost his passion for teaching at the same time he has lost his hair. Hours and hours of low-quality PowerPoints read with a voiceover, providing information repeated over and over without adding value, making the videos three times longer than necessary.

That's when it clicked: there's a lot more room to keep this rigorous and make it actually engaging. So I built this tool around the NEBOSH content itself, my own study notes and case studies, and kept it current with today's H&S laws and guidance — the resource I wish I'd had instead. It's not affiliated with NEBOSH, but every question stays true to the official syllabus — just built to feel less like a lecture and more like the kind of last-minute quizzing you'd do with your mates the night before a school exam.

Does this cover both GIC1 and GIC2?

Yes. Question banks, the scenario simulator and the progress tracker are mapped to both GIC1 (management of health and safety) and GIC2 (risk assessment) as defined in the 2026 syllabus.

What language is it in?

Available in both English and French. The English version matches the language of the official NEBOSH IGC exam; the French version covers the same content for candidates who'd rather revise in French.

Do I still need to take a NEBOSH-accredited training course?

Yes. This is a revision and practice tool, meant to be used after your training, while you're preparing for the exam itself — not a replacement for an accredited course. It's built to consolidate what you've already learned and get you exam-ready.

Yes. If you're not satisfied within the first 14 days of access, you get a full refund, no conditions attached.

How is this different from the free NEBOSH quiz sites?

Most free quiz banks are recycled multiple-choice questions written for the retired IG1/IG2 format, with no marking on written or scenario answers.
