Does the NEBOSH IGC Expire?

Published Jul 19, 2026 · 6 min read

Short version: no, the NEBOSH IGC does not expire. Once you pass it, you hold it for life — no renewal, no re-examination, no CPD hours tied to keeping it valid. What can change over time isn't the certificate's official standing, it's how current an employer assumes your knowledge is if you've been away from HSE work for a long stretch. Those are two different questions, and most of the confusion around "does it expire" comes from mixing them up.

Officially: it never expires

NEBOSH doesn't revoke or time-limit the International General Certificate. There's no periodic reassessment and no mandatory refresher module — pass it once and it's yours indefinitely, recognised the same way by NEBOSH, IOSH, and the employers and regulators who reference it. You can list it on a CV or LinkedIn profile at any point in your career without a "still valid" caveat attached.

Valid vs. valued — the distinction that actually matters

Where people get tripped up is assuming "doesn't expire" means "always carries the same weight." It doesn't, quite. If you passed the IGC in 2012 and haven't worked in health and safety since, the certificate is still technically valid — but a hiring manager in 2026 has a reasonable question: are you current on modern risk management practice, or on whatever's changed since NEBOSH last updated the syllabus? That's not a NEBOSH rule, it's ordinary hiring judgement, and it applies more sharply in sectors where practice moves fast — construction standards, process safety regulation, remote-work risk — than in more static ones.

Key takeaway: the qualification's validity and your market value aren't the same thing. NEBOSH guarantees the first one forever. Only you can keep the second one current.

What NEBOSH IGC compares to

It's worth knowing this isn't how every HSE-adjacent credential works, since some people assume renewal is universal. MASE recertification runs on a 3-year audit cycle. First Aid at Work certificates expire after 3 years and need retraining. CAPM requires PDUs or a re-exam within 3 years. The NEBOSH IGC — like IOSH Managing Safely — sits in the other camp: pass once, hold for life, no renewal mechanism at all.

If you want to keep it competitive, not just valid

None of the following is required by NEBOSH. It's what actually moves the needle if you're returning to HSE work after a gap, or just want a stronger CV alongside an older certificate:

  • Stay active in HSE roles. Continuous practical experience is the single strongest signal to an employer — stronger than any paperwork.
  • Pursue the next qualification up. A NEBOSH Diploma, or a sector-specific module like NEBOSH Construction, shows progression rather than a credential frozen at one point in time.
  • Document CPD, even informally. Webinars, training, project work — not mandatory, but worth tracking so you have something concrete to point to.
  • Take a refresher if you've had a long gap. Not because the certificate needs it, but because regulatory guidance and best practice genuinely do shift over a decade.

FAQ

Can I still put NEBOSH IGC on my CV if I passed it years ago?

Yes. The qualification never expires and employers recognise it indefinitely. Pairing it with evidence of recent HSE experience or CPD helps if there's been a long gap.

Do I need to renew my NEBOSH IGC every few years?

No. Unlike MASE recertification or a CAPM re-exam, NEBOSH has no renewal cycle, CPD hour requirement, or refresher module tied to the IGC. It's a one-time, lifetime qualification.

If I haven't worked in HSE for years, is my NEBOSH IGC still valid?

Officially yes — it's still valid. Employers may still ask about recent practical experience, so a refresher course or documented CPD can help demonstrate current knowledge if you're returning after a break.

Does a NEBOSH syllabus update make my older IGC outdated?

No — your certificate itself doesn't become invalid when NEBOSH updates the syllabus, as happened with the 2026 GIC1/GIC2 rename. The core content stays recognised; it's worth knowing what changed if you're advising others or returning to formal study.

Is the NEBOSH IGC recognised internationally without renewal?

Yes. It's accepted across the UK, Middle East, Asia, Australia and other regions without any renewal requirement attached.
