Does the NEBOSH IGC Expire?

Complete 2026 guide to certification validity, renewal requirements, and what you actually need to do to keep your credential valued by employers.

The short answer

No, the NEBOSH IGC does not expire. Once you pass the International General Certificate exam, you hold that qualification for life. NEBOSH does not revoke or expire your certification based on time alone.

However—and this is important—there's a critical distinction between three things:

  • The qualification itself (which never expires)
  • Its relevance and employability (which can diminish over time)
  • Regulatory recognition (which depends on keeping knowledge current)

Understanding this distinction matters significantly if you're using the qualification for employment, freelance work, or professional positioning. Let's dig into each.

How long is the NEBOSH IGC valid for?

The NEBOSH IGC is valid for life from the date you pass the exam. There is no renewal requirement, no periodic reassessment, and no "refresher" module mandated by NEBOSH.

This differs from some other HSE certifications and professional credentials that require:

  • Periodic renewals (e.g., MASE recertification every 3 years)
  • Continuing professional development (CPD) hours
  • Re-examination after a set period

NEBOSH takes a different approach: your IGC remains officially recognized indefinitely. You never have to sit the exam again, never have to pay a renewal fee, and never have to complete mandatory CPD to keep your qualification "active."

The gap between "valid" and "valued"

Here's where many NEBOSH IGC holders encounter confusion. While your certification never officially expires, employers and clients may view outdated qualifications skeptically if:

  • Your qualification is more than 10–15 years old
  • HSE regulations or best practices have significantly evolved
  • You cannot demonstrate current knowledge of your field
  • You've had no recent practical experience in health and safety

Why this matters

If you passed the NEBOSH IGC in 2010 and haven't worked in HSE since then, your qualification is technically still valid—but a prospective employer in 2026 might reasonably question whether you're current on modern risk management practices, digital safety tools, or recent legislative changes.

This is especially true in specialized sectors like:

  • Construction safety — with evolving building standards
  • Chemical and process safety — changing regulations
  • Occupational health management — new ergonomic standards
  • Remote/hybrid work safety — relatively new domain

In these cases, your IGC is still recognized—but supplementing it with current, relevant experience or additional qualifications makes your candidacy much stronger.

NEBOSH IGC validity for different purposes

1. Professional Qualification Recognition

Your NEBOSH IGC is recognized indefinitely as a professional qualification by:

  • IOSH (Institution of Occupational Safety and Health)
  • Industry bodies in the UK, Middle East, Asia, and beyond
  • Multinational corporations with standardized HSE requirements

You can list it on your CV, LinkedIn profile, and professional documents without any time limit.

2. Employment and Freelance Work

Employers' views vary based on organizational size and HSE maturity:

  • Large organizations (ISO 45001 certified): Often recognize NEBOSH IGC indefinitely but may prefer candidates with recent practical experience
  • Mid-sized companies: May require supplementary training or evidence of continuous learning
  • Regulatory bodies: Typically recognize NEBOSH IGC without expiration, though they may require proof of continuing competence in your specific role

3. Regulatory Compliance

For HSE-regulated roles (e.g., Competent Person for specific hazards), your NEBOSH IGC demonstrates baseline competence. However:

  • Regulatory bodies may require evidence of continuous, relevant experience
  • Sector-specific regulations might mandate additional qualifications
  • Role-specific competence requirements may supersede general NEBOSH credentials

Do you need to renew your NEBOSH IGC?

Officially: No. NEBOSH does not require renewal, re-examination, or CPD hours tied to your IGC. There's no certificate to reprint, no fee to pay, and no box to tick.

Practically: It depends. To keep your qualification valued by employers and clients, consider these steps.

Recommended best practices

  • Stay active in HSE roles — Continuous practical experience demonstrates ongoing competence
  • Pursue advanced qualifications — NEBOSH Diplomas, CAPM, or MASE certification show career progression
  • Document CPD activities — Even though not mandatory, track professional development, training, and project work
  • Keep knowledge current — Follow HSE guidance updates, attend industry webinars, or take refresher courses
  • Specialize further — Gain recognized qualifications in your specific sector (e.g., NEBOSH Construction, Fire & Rescue)

By doing these things, you're not renewing your IGC—you're ensuring it remains a credible, valued credential in your professional positioning.

If your NEBOSH IGC is "outdated," what should you do?

If you hold a NEBOSH IGC from several years ago and want to strengthen your professional position, you have several clear paths forward.

Option 1: Pursue the NEBOSH Diploma

The Diploma in Environmental, Health and Safety Management is the advanced credential. It demonstrates specialized knowledge and is highly valued by employers. No formal "upgrade" path exists—you'd take the Diploma as a new qualification.

Option 2: Take NEBOSH specialization modules

NEBOSH offers sector-specific qualifications that complement your IGC and show focused expertise:

  • NEBOSH Construction (NCC1/NCC2)
  • NEBOSH Fire & Rescue
  • NEBOSH Oil and Gas
  • NEBOSH Process Safety Management

Option 3: Pursue CAPM or project management certification

If you're moving toward a leadership role, CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management) or PMP pairs well with an HSE background and keeps your credentials contemporary.

Option 4: Document continuous professional development

  • Attend industry conferences and training
  • Complete manufacturer-specific courses (e.g., confined space rescue, fall protection systems)
  • Lead safety initiatives and document outcomes
  • Take online courses in emerging areas (e.g., AI-driven safety, mental health at work)

Key takeaways: NEBOSH IGC expiration rules

  1. The NEBOSH IGC does not expire — You hold the qualification for life
  2. No renewal is required — Unlike MASE or CAPM, there's no mandatory refresher
  3. Official recognition is indefinite — NEBOSH, IOSH, and regulatory bodies recognize it indefinitely
  4. Market relevance may change — Employers may value recent experience alongside your qualification
  5. Continuous learning is optional but recommended — To stay competitive, keep your knowledge current
  6. You can list it on your CV forever — There's no need to remove it or update it based on age

FAQ: NEBOSH IGC Expiration and Validity

Can I still put NEBOSH IGC on my CV if I passed it 10 years ago?

Yes, absolutely. Your qualification never expires, and employers recognize it indefinitely. However, pair it with evidence of continuous professional experience or additional qualifications to demonstrate current competence.

Do I need to take a refresher course after a certain number of years?

No, NEBOSH does not mandate refresher training. However, if you've been away from HSE work for many years, a refresher course is advisable to update your knowledge of regulatory changes.

Does my NEBOSH IGC need periodic renewal, like some other certifications do?

No. The NEBOSH IGC has no renewal cycle, no recertification audit, and no expiry date. Once you pass, it's yours for life — you don't need to do anything to keep it valid.

If I haven't worked in HSE for 5 years, is my NEBOSH IGC still valid?

Officially, yes—your certification is still valid. However, employers may question your current knowledge. Consider taking a refresher or pursuing a complementary qualification to demonstrate your commitment to staying current.

Will my NEBOSH IGC be outdated if HSE regulations change?

Your qualification itself doesn't become invalid, but your knowledge might be incomplete. NEBOSH IGC covers core HSE principles that remain relevant. For regulatory updates, follow guidance from the HSE website or take sector-specific training.

Do I need the Diploma if I already have the IGC?

No, the Diploma is not mandatory. However, if you want to advance your career or demonstrate deeper expertise, the Diploma is valuable. The IGC alone is sufficient for many HSE roles.

Can employers reject my NEBOSH IGC because it's too old?

Technically, no—the qualification is officially recognized indefinitely. However, employers can require evidence of current competence. If that's a concern, document your continuous professional development or pursue an advanced qualification.

Is the NEBOSH IGC recognized internationally if I haven't renewed it?

Yes. The NEBOSH IGC is recognized globally without any renewal requirement. It's accepted in the UK, Middle East, Asia, Australia, and many other regions.
