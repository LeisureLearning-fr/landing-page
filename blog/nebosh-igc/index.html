Does the NEBOSH IGC Expire?

Jul 19, 2026 · 6 min read

Short answer: no. Once you pass the NEBOSH International General Certificate, it's yours for life — no renewal, no re-sit, no expiry date printed on the certificate. But "never expires" and "still gets you the job" aren't quite the same thing, so it's worth understanding both sides.

The short answer: the NEBOSH IGC does not expire. NEBOSH does not revoke or time-limit the qualification once you've passed it.

Where it gets more nuanced is the difference between three separate things:

  • The qualification itself — never expires
  • Its relevance and employability — can diminish over time
  • Regulatory recognition — depends on keeping knowledge current

That distinction matters if you're using the qualification for employment, freelance work, or professional positioning. Here's each one in turn.

How long is the NEBOSH IGC valid for?

The NEBOSH IGC is valid for life from the date you pass the exam. There's no renewal requirement, no periodic reassessment, and no mandatory "refresher" module.

That's a different model to some other HSE certifications, which do require:

  • Periodic renewals (e.g., MASE recertification every 3 years)
  • Continuing professional development (CPD) hours
  • Re-examination after a set period

NEBOSH doesn't work that way. Your IGC stays officially recognized indefinitely — no exam to re-sit, no fee to keep it "active."

The gap between "valid" and "valued"

This is where most of the confusion actually sits. While your certification never officially expires, employers and clients can still view an older qualification skeptically if:

  • It's more than 10–15 years old
  • HSE regulations or best practices have moved on significantly since
  • You can't demonstrate current knowledge of your field
  • You've had no recent practical HSE experience

Why this matters in practice

If you passed the NEBOSH IGC in 2010 and haven't worked in HSE since, the qualification is technically still valid — but a hiring manager in 2026 might reasonably ask whether you're current on modern risk management practices, digital safety tools, or recent legislative changes.

This shows up more in specialized sectors, where the underlying rules move faster than the syllabus does:

  • Construction safety — evolving building standards
  • Chemical and process safety — changing regulations
  • Occupational health management — newer ergonomic standards
  • Remote/hybrid work safety — a genuinely new domain

Your IGC is still recognized in all of these — but pairing it with current, relevant experience makes a much stronger case than the certificate on its own.

NEBOSH IGC validity, by purpose

1. Professional qualification recognition

Your NEBOSH IGC is recognized indefinitely as a professional qualification by IOSH, industry bodies across the UK, Middle East, and Asia, and multinational corporations with standardized HSE requirements. You can list it on your CV or LinkedIn without any time limit.

2. Employment and freelance work

Employers' views vary by size and HSE maturity:

  • Large, ISO 45001-certified organizations — usually recognize it indefinitely, but may still prefer candidates with recent practical experience
  • Mid-sized companies — may ask for supplementary training or evidence of continuous learning
  • Regulatory bodies — typically recognize it without expiration, though some require proof of continuing competence for specific roles

3. Regulatory compliance

For HSE-regulated roles (e.g., Competent Person for a specific hazard), the IGC demonstrates baseline competence. But regulators may still ask for evidence of continuous, relevant experience, and sector-specific rules can require additional qualifications on top.

Do you need to renew your NEBOSH IGC?

Officially: no. No renewal, no re-examination, no CPD hours tied to keeping it valid. There's no certificate to reprint, no fee to pay, and no box to tick.

Practically: it depends — on how you want the qualification to read to an employer. To keep it valued rather than just valid, worth considering:

  • Stay active in HSE roles — continuous practical experience is the strongest signal of current competence
  • Pursue advanced qualifications — the NEBOSH Diploma, CAPM, or MASE certification show career progression
  • Document CPD activities — not mandatory, but worth tracking training and project work anyway
  • Keep knowledge current — follow HSE guidance updates, industry webinars, or refresher courses
  • Specialize further — sector-specific NEBOSH qualifications (Construction, Fire & Rescue) show focused expertise

If your NEBOSH IGC feels outdated, what to do about it

If you hold an IGC from several years back and want to strengthen your position, there are a few clear paths.

Pursue the NEBOSH Diploma

The Diploma in Environmental, Health and Safety Management is the advanced credential — highly valued by employers. There's no formal "upgrade" path from the IGC; you'd sit the Diploma as a new qualification in its own right.

Take a specialization module

NEBOSH offers sector-specific qualifications that complement the IGC: Construction (NCC1/NCC2), Fire & Rescue, Oil and Gas, Process Safety Management.

Pair it with project management credentials

If you're moving toward a leadership role, CAPM or PMP pairs naturally with an HSE background and signals your credentials are current.

Document your CPD, even informally

Conference attendance, manufacturer-specific training (confined space rescue, fall protection systems), safety initiatives you've led, or newer courses in emerging areas — all of it builds the case alongside the certificate itself.

Key takeaways

  1. The NEBOSH IGC does not expire — it's a lifetime qualification
  2. No renewal is required — unlike MASE or CAPM, there's no mandatory refresher
  3. Official recognition is indefinite — NEBOSH, IOSH, and regulatory bodies all recognize it without a time limit
  4. Market relevance can still shift — employers may weigh recent experience alongside the qualification
  5. Continuous learning is optional but worth it — it's what keeps the certificate competitive, not compliant
  6. You can list it on your CV indefinitely — no need to remove or caveat it based on age

FAQ: NEBOSH IGC expiration and validity

Can I still put NEBOSH IGC on my CV if I passed it 10 years ago?

Yes. Your qualification never expires, and employers recognize it indefinitely. It's still worth pairing it with evidence of continuous professional experience or additional qualifications to show current competence.

Do I need to take a refresher course after a certain number of years?

NEBOSH doesn't mandate one. But if you've been away from HSE work for many years, a refresher is genuinely useful for catching up on regulatory changes — worth doing even though it's not required.

Does my NEBOSH IGC need periodic renewal, like some other certifications do?

No. The NEBOSH IGC has no renewal cycle, no recertification audit, and no expiry date. Once you pass, it's yours for life — you don't need to do anything to keep it valid.

If I haven't worked in HSE for 5 years, is my NEBOSH IGC still valid?

Officially, yes. Employers may still question how current your knowledge is, though — a refresher or a complementary qualification can help demonstrate you've kept up.

Will my NEBOSH IGC be outdated if HSE regulations change?

The qualification itself doesn't become invalid, but your knowledge of specific regulations might lag. The core principles the IGC covers stay relevant — for regulatory updates specifically, HSE guidance or sector-specific training fills the gap.

Do I need the Diploma if I already have the IGC?

No, it's not required. The IGC is sufficient for many HSE roles on its own. The Diploma is worth pursuing if you want to advance into more senior or specialized positions.

Can employers reject my NEBOSH IGC because it's too old?

Not on the basis of the qualification itself — it's recognized indefinitely. Employers can still ask for evidence of current competence, which is where documenting CPD or pursuing an advanced qualification helps.

Is the NEBOSH IGC recognized internationally if I haven't renewed it?

Yes — recognized globally with no renewal requirement, across the UK, Middle East, Asia, Australia, and beyond.
