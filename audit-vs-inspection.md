# Audit or Inspection? A Simple Test for Telling Them Apart

*Meta description: The real difference between an audit and an inspection in health and safety — frequency, scope, who does it, and what comes out the other end.*
*Suggested keywords: audit vs inspection difference, internal vs external audit health and safety, NEBOSH monitoring and measuring*
*Angle: exam-tips*

In everyday conversation, "audit" and "inspection" get used almost interchangeably. In an exam, treating them as the same thing costs marks — they're different tools, used at different frequencies, producing different kinds of output.

## Inspection: quick, physical, frequent

An inspection is a straightforward visual check of a workplace and the activities in it at a particular point in time — is the guard on, is the floor clear, is PPE being worn. It takes a short time to complete, happens relatively often (monthly or quarterly is typical), can usually be done by one trained internal person, and produces a short checklist-style output flagging specific physical issues.

## Audit: slow, document-heavy, infrequent

An audit is a thorough examination of the entire documented health and safety management system and how it's actually working in practice — not just "is the guard on" but "is there a functioning process that ensures guards stay on." It takes much longer, happens far less often (annually is common), needs a higher level of skill to carry out properly, and produces a comprehensive report covering the system's strengths and weaknesses, not just a list of physical faults.

## The comparison, side by side

| | Inspection | Audit |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Quick | Slow |
| Frequency | Often (e.g. every 3 months) | Rare (e.g. every 12 months) |
| Who does it | Internal worker | Internal or external auditor |
| Skill required | Lower | Higher |
| Output | Short checklist, physical issues | Full report, system-level findings |
| Resources needed | Minimal, one person | Significant, may need a team |

## Internal vs external audit — a second layer of confusion

Once you've separated audit from inspection, there's a second distinction sitting inside "audit" itself. An **internal** audit uses the organisation's own staff — cheaper, and they know the workplace intimately, but less independent. An **external** audit is done by someone outside the organisation — costs more, but brings independence, broader legal awareness, and a fresh perspective that can catch things insiders have stopped noticing.

The tempting wrong answer here is assuming internal audits are "more thorough" because the auditor knows the site better. Familiarity isn't the same as independence — and independence is precisely what an internal auditor structurally can't offer, however good they are at their job.

## Why the distinction actually matters day to day

A site that only ever gets inspected and never audited will catch broken guards and missing signage — real, useful findings — but will never catch a management system that's quietly stopped functioning underneath all those individually-fine physical details. Both tools are necessary; neither substitutes for the other.
